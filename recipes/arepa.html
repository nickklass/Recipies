The Arepa, Typical Food In Venezuela And Colombia

In every country, there is a typical thing the locals eat for breakfast on a daily basis. This is the Arepa. Typical in Venezuela and Colombia. Below, you can find the recipe. Simple and delicious.

Using Corn Meal flour, water and elbow greese, you too can make this dish.

Ingredients

  • Some warm water. (enough will do)
  • Some cornmeal flour. (Promasa, Do√±a Arepa are good brands)
  • Some sugar. (not too much)
  • Some salt. (to taste)

Instructions

After washing your hands, Slowly mix the ingredients together until you have a nice ball that stays together and is very maleable. You want it to ba able to mould the dough without it cracking (not enough water) and not so soft where the dough is sticking to your hands. It has to be just right.

Plut your pan on the fire with some oil or butter. If you do it right, you should not even need the oil or the butter after the first times making them. Mould the dough into a flying saucer disk shape and place it onto the pan. Flip when golden brown.

The fun comes where you create and add your fillings. Go wild or simple. Cheese is good. Cheese with avocado is better. What about meat? Or fish, beans. Whatever your heart desires. May your tastebuds enjoy this badly explained food.
